# LinkedIn Draft 06: Status Is A Bad Memory

I still use status columns.

They are practical.

`approved` tells me where a record is now.

It says almost nothing about how it got there.

Who approved it?

What evidence existed?

Did the operator change anything?

Did the record skip a step?

Was there a failed export before the successful one?

That is why I like treating status as a projection.

The real memory lives in events:

extracted,

reviewed,

blocked,

confirmed,

exported,

retried,

failed.

The screen can still show the simple status. People need that.

But when something breaks, the system needs a story with timestamps and actors, not a single mutable word pretending to remember everything.

I still show status on the screen.

I make events do the remembering.
