# LinkedIn Draft 10: From Admin Table To Operator Workspace

An admin table is always nearby.

Rows.

Filters.

Actions.

Badges, if we are feeling responsible.

I have built plenty of them.

Then you watch an operator use the product and the table starts to look lazy.

They are not looking for "all records."

They want blocked work.

They want the reason it is blocked.

They want to know what changed.

They want the next safe action.

That calls for a workspace with opinions.

Prioritize exceptions.

Show stale data as stale.

Make degraded state visible.

Hide actions outside the role.

At that point, the table has turned into a workbench.

It tells the operator what they can safely do next.

If the operator has to reconstruct the workflow from a generic table, the product handed them homework.

A good workspace gives the next safe move.
