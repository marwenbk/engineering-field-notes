# LinkedIn Draft 17: The Revert Wave Was The Mature Move

I used to see big cleanup diffs as progress.

Now I get nervous when a diff fixes too many kinds of problems at once.

Security fix.

Generated client change.

Package cleanup.

CI update.

Formatting.

Static-analysis noise.

Maybe each change is useful.

Together, the review gets blurry.

The mature move is sometimes to revert the wave and keep the real fixes.

That feels annoying in the moment.

It also recovers signal.

A reviewer should understand what changed and what risk they are accepting.

Useful work gets buried when the diff becomes too broad.

I trust smaller changes more now:

one behavior fix,

one security fix,

one generated-client update tied to a contract change,

one packaging cleanup with its own proof.

The review gets boring again.

That is a compliment.
